Building and Verifying a Simple Circuit

Learn the basics of Logisim by building and verifying a simple combinational circuit.

Make sure that you have already downloaded and installed Logisim 3.8.0 on your computer. Follow Downloading Logisim 3.8.0 if you haven't.
Logisim might not be an intuitive tool at first glance. This quick guide aims to guide a beginner user through the basic features of Logisim through building and verifying a simple three-input majority voting circuit, which outputs 1 when a majority of inputs are 1 and 0 otherwise.
  1. Open Logisim.
  2. Follow Adding a Dip Switch to add a dip switch to handle three inputs.
  3. Follow Adding the Logic Unit to create the logic unit.
  4. Follow Adding an LED to add an LED to visualize the output of the circuit.
  5. Follow Wiring the Majority Voting Circuit to wire the circuit.
  6. Verify the circuit.
    1. In the top bar, click to switch to poke mode.
    2. Click on the individual switches to change the state of the circuit.
      Note: You should only see the LED light up when any two or three of the switches are flipped on.
      Note: Click on the wires to see the value that they are currently carrying.
Congratulations! You now know how to navigate the basic menus of Logisim and how to use the two main interaction modes (select and poke) to edit and verify a combinational circuit.
