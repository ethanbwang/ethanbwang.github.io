Simulating a Circuit Using the Simulate Menu

Learn how to use the simulate menu to verify a basic sequential circuit. This short guide walks you through building and verifying a synchronous counter.

After creating a sequential/synchronous circuit, the most intuitive way to verify that it works is to switch to poke mode and manually click on the clock. However, this does not take full advantage of Logisim's features.
  1. Open a new circuit in Logisim.
  2. Follow Building a Synchronous Counter to have a sequential circuit ready to verify.
  3. Set the design/simulate menu to the Simulate menu.
  4. Set the properties/state menu to the State menu.
    Note: You will be able to see a register's current value in the state menu or above the register and below the register label in circuit view.
  5. In the Simulate menu, click the full tick button .
    You will see that the value contained in R0 is now 1 instead of 0.
  6. In the menu bar > Simulate, change the auto tick frequency to 8.0 Hz.


  7. In the Simulate menu, click the auto tick button .
    The counter should now be automatically counting.
  8. Click the auto tick button again to stop auto ticking.
  9. In the Simulate menu, click the pause button .
  10. In the Simulate menu, click the full tick button .
    Blue dots should have showed up between the clock and the register showing where the signal currently is.

  11. In the Simulate menu, click the step button .
    You will see that blue dots are now between the register's Q output and the adder's first input. This shows the signal propagation as a result of stepping forward once.

  12. In the Simulate menu, click the play button to resume automatic signal propagation.
Congratulations! You now know how to use the simulate menu to step through simulating your circuit. You can keep playing around with the simulate menu to get used to the features it provides.
